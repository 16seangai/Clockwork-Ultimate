Clockwork

About

Clockwork is the men's club ultimate team affiliated with Princeton University. The team offers two levels of commitment. The A-team is a regionally-competitive program that placed second at sectionals and lost in pre-quarters of regionals. The B-team offers a setting to play ultimate with less time commitment but still offers a similar competition level, even qualifying for A-team regionals as recently as three years ago. Both share a common goal — to improve and grow as a team and build towards a strong spring season. All members, regardless of team, become part of a fun-loving community that hosts joint pregames and wholesome fun time events with the girls team.

Frosh Week Pickup

Whether you've played since middle school or have no experience at all, come out to pickup during frosh week. Times vary, but we usually start playing in the afternoon at 3 or 4. Details forthcoming. Make sure to bring water, a light and a dark shirt, and cleats (or running shoes).

Tryouts

Tryouts are held in the week following frosh week pickup. The process consists of two or three practices followed by a tournament over the weekend. Rosters are announced in the week after the tournament. Tryout dates will be posted soon.

Practice

The A-team practices 4 times a week with 2-hour practices usually held on Mondays, Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays. The B-team practices 3 times a week on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays.

In the fall and spring, when the weather is nicer, practices are usually held in the afternoonfrom 4:45pm - 6:45pm at West Windsor. In the winter and early spring, practices are held on turf at various times at Campbell-Finney or Sherrerd.

Tournaments

In the fall, both teams attend three or four warm-up tournaments to gear up for the spring season. The spring is highlighted by an optional week-long spring break trip to Myrtle Beach where the A-team plays at a tournament called Oak Creek invite and then both teams play at a fun, relaxed tournament known as High Tide. After one or two more weekend scrimmages or local tournaments, the teams compete in the USAU postseason series.
